## Purpose
Records all trees on GP and gram sabha land — species, count, annual yield, and income from auction/harvest. Trees are GP assets and a source of revenue.

## Key Fields
- Survey number/plot (from N24 — GP land); Location
- Species name (common and botanical); Number of trees (species-wise, girth-class)
- Year of planting (if plantation); Estimated annual yield (fruit/timber/wood)
- Income from harvest/auction (date, amount, receipt reference via N7)
- Trees lost (date, cause: storm/disease/theft/natural; replaced Y/N)
- Trees planted as replacement; Remarks (protected species, court orders)

## Validation Rules
- Tree count must agree with physical count at annual survey
- Unauthorised felling without GP resolution + forest NOC = major objection and criminal offence
- Tree auction income must be receipted via N7 and posted to N5 immediately
- Tree auction must be by public tender — private sale not permitted
- Trees in N33 must correspond to land parcels in N24

## Audit Risk
MEDIUM — Common objections:
- Trees felled without GP resolution or forest dept. clearance
- Auction income not receipted or cash pocketed
- Register not updated after storm/disease losses
