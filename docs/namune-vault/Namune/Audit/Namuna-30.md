## Purpose
The MASTER register tracking ALL audit objections across years — their status, recovery details, and final closure. While N27 is the monthly monitoring report, N30 is the cumulative compliance register used by CEO ZP and external auditors to assess the GP's audit health over time. This is the first document examined in any superior audit or CAG inspection.

## Key Fields
- Audit report year and reference no.; Auditor/audit party name
- Objection number; Para heading
- Amount involved; Nature (recoverable / procedural / documentary)
- Date raised; GP reply date and summary
- Auditor acceptance (date, reply reference)
- Recovery amount deposited (date, N7 receipt no.)
- Documents produced; Closure order reference
- Status (open / closed / appealed); Remarks (escalated to director / court)

## Validation Rules
- Every para in every audit report must have an entry — gaps indicate suppression of objections
- Closed objections must have documentary evidence of compliance (recovery receipt, corrected entry reference)
- Recording "closed" without actual deposit of recoverable amount is false compliance
- Objections pending beyond 3 years require CEO ZP personal review [VERIFY: escalation norms]
- This register is first examined in CAG/superior audit — incompleteness draws adverse comment

## Audit Risk
VERY HIGH — This IS the audit register; its incompleteness is itself a major objection:
- Long-pending objections (5+ years) unresolved
- Objections closed without actual compliance evidence
- Register not updated after audit visits
- Recovery amounts "deposited" in N30 but not reflected in N7/N5
