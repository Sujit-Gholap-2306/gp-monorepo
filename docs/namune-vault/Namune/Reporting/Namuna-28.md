## Purpose
Tracks compliance with the statutory requirement that GPs spend at least 15% of their budget on SC/ST welfare and 10% on women welfare. Non-compliance is a major audit and political objection — the most commonly flagged social compliance issue in GP audits.

## Key Fields
- Financial year; Total GP budget/receipts (base for % calculation)
- 15% earmark for SC/ST welfare (calculated amount)
- Actual expenditure on SC/ST welfare (scheme-wise — roads in SC area, water in SC wadi, scholarships, etc.)
- % of SC/ST target met
- 10% earmark for women welfare (calculated amount)
- Actual expenditure on women welfare (scheme-wise — anganwadi, SHG support, women sanitation)
- % of women welfare target met; Balance yet to be spent; Certification

## Validation Rules
- Expenditure claimed as SC/ST welfare must actually serve SC/ST population — non-targeted works incorrectly claimed are major objections
- Budget provision for earmarks must exist in N1
- Expenditure posted here must reconcile with N26ख entries
- Year-end: if target not met, surplus funds carried forward for earmarked purpose next year [VERIFY: carryover rule]
- GPs unable to meet targets must document reasons — unexplained non-compliance is escalated

## Audit Risk
VERY HIGH — Most common statutory compliance objection:
- 15% and/or 10% targets not met at year-end
- Works incorrectly classified as SC/ST/women welfare without beneficiary targeting evidence
- Budget provision not made for earmarks in N1
